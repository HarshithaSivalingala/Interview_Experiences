[META TPM Screening Round]

Hey everyone, I recently completed a Meta TPM phone screen. Sharing everything while it's fresh. Hope this helps someone.

*BASICS*
Role: TPM (IC5 level)
Format: 45 min phone screen
Interviewer: A Principal TPM (NOT the hiring manager, Meta uses a screener pool)
Structure: 1 min intro → 2 min background → 4 deep-dive blocks → 2 min Q&A
No system design, no case study: pure behavioral with heavy technical probing
Outcome: Called for full loop.

IMPORTANT: This is a general pipeline screen. You're NOT matched to a team yet. Team matching happens after the onsite.

*QUESTIONS ASKED:*

*INTRO*
1. Quick background on last couple of years (keep to 90 sec!)

*BLOCK 1 — Technical Decision-Making (~10 min)*
2. Instance where you leveraged technical expertise to make a solution decision?
3. What was the specific decision?
4. Walk me through the architecture choices
5. Any system constraints?
6. Tell me about the reliability of the system
7. How did you measure and maintain reliability? (they specifically named Lambda, S3, DynamoDB, caches — want you to talk failure modes)

*BLOCK 2 — Zero-to-One Program (~12 min)*
8. Walk me through a DIFFERENT program from ideation to launch
9. How did you narrow down which problems to solve?
10. How did you track execution across teams?
11. Specific risk that was a critical blocker + how you mitigated it
12. Summarize the mitigation (asked because my answer was too long)
13. What status reporting did you do?
14. What was the outcome of the A/B test?

*BLOCK 3 — Leadership & Stakeholders (~8 min)*
15. Challenging scenario with leadership?
16. How did you navigate the conflict between two parties?
17. How did you present it? How long? What alignments? How did you write the story?
18. What did you learn?

*BLOCK 4 — Crisis Management (~8 min)*
19. Late-breaking technical issue that blocked a release in <1 week?
20. What did you have to explain to leadership?
21. How did you present it? (they push for specifics)
22. How did you keep leadership engaged + outcome?
