[TPM Interview Experience] 
Databricks.

Six rounds:

1. HM Screen: Deep dive into your most complex program. Questions on demand forecasting (organic vs. inorganic), resource optimization (GPU bin packing, utilization), and customer migration complexity. They want systems-level thinking, not just execution recaps.
2. Behavioral / Partnerships Fit (Sr. Director): STAR format questions on stakeholder management, vendor contingency planning, and cost-benefit tradeoffs. Asked about times you had to pivot when a vendor dependency fell through and how you managed cost implications. Have 2-3 complete STAR stories with quantified outcomes ready.
3. Technical Deep Dive (Sr. Staff SWE): They go deep. Asked to whiteboard full system architecture end-to-end: pipeline design, latency tradeoffs, model evaluation frameworks, embedding strategies. Also covered experimentation rigor and how you challenge reported metrics. If you've built ML/AI pipelines, know every component.
4. Eng Fit / Program Management (Sr. Director): Full lifecycle walkthrough of a complex program from inception to delivery. Dug into stakeholder mapping across eng/product/ops, how you handled vendor disruptions or timeline shifts, modular architecture decisions, and navigating conflicting priorities across teams (science vs. product vs. engineering tradeoffs).
5. Team Fit & Leadership: Tests leadership through ambiguity, not just execution. Got a scenario-based diagnostic question ("Google Search results dropped 10%, walk me through how you'd debug it"). Have a structured framework ready to volunteer unprompted. Also asked about "why Databricks" and how you use AI/agentic tools in your workflow.
6. Technical Release Management: Asked about release management for model launches, model handoff processes and how you'd streamline them, reliability/latency tradeoffs in production systems, and how you influence engineering when there's pushback. They care a lot about commercial instincts here: provisioned throughput, capacity math, pricing/costing, deprecation strategies, and customer migration planning. Expect questions about pace too (Day Zero launches, what happens when you miss a launch window by even a day).
